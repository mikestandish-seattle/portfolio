Who I Am

I'm a creative director, writer, editor, and strategist. But over a career spanning agency and in-house, a more honest job title might be: a creative leader who figures out how to get things done.

This comes from working in experiential and event marketing, a field that comprises many disciplines, and often requires people to dive in, even if it's outside of their comfort zone.

That's me: I'm not afraid to dive in. And follow where the story takes me.

First, I Wrote Shipping News

Rather than the novels and screenplays I thought I'd write out of college, I got a job writing shipping news for Marine Digest magazine. While others were moving to Seattle to work at a place called Amazon, I was happy to make $8 an hour to write about ballast water and invasive species to an audience of roughly 2,000 people interested in West Coast ports.

I found the job by opening the phone book and calling every newspaper and magazine to see if they wanted to hire me, a fresh college grad with no professional writing experience. It turns out they did.

What I Learned

  • How to work in a (very small) newsroom
  • Where to look for stories
  • How to get people to open up about what they're passionate about
  • Ballast water contains invasive species

I Edited Technical Manuals

As the dot-com era was ramping up, I spent six months copy-editing an HTML how-to book. The series with the cute black and white animal drawings on the covers.

What I Learned

  • How to get quickly up to speed on technical subject matter
  • How to adhere to a style guide, consistently and accurately

I Went to Disney

I started at the Walt Disney Internet Group as a copy editor at two daily entertainment news sites. Then I became a staff writer. Then an editor. Then the dot-com crash changed everything.

Our staff was cut. Then cut again. One of our sites was shuttered. In the middle of all this, we decided to launch a brand-new site, Movies.com. As we clawed our way out of the crash, our site traffic grew. I became a senior editor, a columnist, a video maker. Our team was small, which meant I got to do all the things. It was heaven.

What I Learned

  • Tech companies should have revenue models
  • How to run a tight editorial calendar
  • How to interview famous people and review albums
  • How to write a weekly column
  • How to build and launch a product
  • That I didn't want to move to Los Angeles to advance my career

I Became a Creative Director

I answered a mysterious Craigslist ad for a position that sounded exactly like me and had no idea what I was getting myself into. I didn't even know what a creative director did. I ended up doing it for almost 11 years. It's my vocation and the thing I'm best at.

During my tenure at PBJS we grew from a 20-person indie shop to a 60-person Publicis agency. Our biggest client was Microsoft. I was thrown into the deep end and found myself writing, directing videos, doing exec presentations, building a traveling museum—you name it. It felt like home.

What I Learned

  • How to have empathy for clients and stakeholders
  • How to rally a team around an idea
  • The value of ideas that can be executed
  • How to create an ecosystem filled with stories that make sense together
  • How to be a conduit between and translator for various disciplines
  • The value of the skills of a generalist
  • How to lead a team and manage people

The Gates Foundation Called

I had a unique opportunity to move from tech and experiential marketing to global health and development communications. I was tasked with building and evolving a creative team in the comms department. Implementing new processes and working with better agencies. In short: upping the Foundation's creative game.

What I Learned

  • How to become a subject matter expert, and fast
  • How to salvage team morale and get back on track
  • The value of process, and the right amount of it
  • How to create content for global markets
  • The usefulness of measurement and research
  • How to convince people on different teams to all work from and contribute to a single content calendar

Copilot+ PC Launch & Build 2025

Overview

Microsoft

Brand Love Activations

Overview

Working with the event design team, I served as writer and editor for brand love activations throughout the year aimed at increasing brand affinity for Microsoft with photo opportunities and interactive content experiences.

What I Did

Recent activations centered on AI and Microsoft's four AI for Good commitments. I wrote quizzes on the history of AI, sourced and reimagined AI for Good blogs as physical brand activations, and wrote copy to explain complex tech concepts to event attendees at a glance.

Key Learning

Interactive experiences at events have to earn their attention. Creating an experience that is eye-catching yet substantive requires understanding what attendees truly care about and taking complicated subject matter and boiling it down to its essence.

Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation

Goalkeepers at the United Nations

Overview

Started by the Gates Foundation, Goalkeepers is a catalyst for action toward the 17 Global Goals for Sustainable Development—a two-day event at the UN General Assembly featuring world leaders including Prime Minister Justin Trudeau, President Barack Obama, and Malala Yousafzai.

My Role

Worked as creative lead on the print data report, videos, and website, and as creative consultant on event and campaign strategy. I edited and supervised production of the foundation's first ever data report, led teams across Europe, Africa, and China, and consulted on all creative elements of the UN event.

Key Learning

I came to understand the value of being the person who builds bridges between teams, whether finding the right agency to work with an in-house team or ensuring internal stakeholders are aligned with campaign strategy.

Bill & Melinda Gates Foundation

Campaign Content

Overview

The Foundation comprises 29 different strategy teams. The external comms team works with all of them to program videos, data visualizations, and content to support their goals across channels, while overseeing the use of the Foundation's brand and tone-of-voice.

What I Did

I treated strategy teams like clients and vendors as an extension of our content team, allowing our small team to operate globally, creating content for audiences in the US, Canada, UK, France, Germany, China, India, and South Africa. I led development from writing scripts and blogs to selecting vendors and steering them through execution of videos and data visualizations.

Key Learning

How to get up to speed quickly on a variety of subjects, understand communication styles that perform best on different regional channels, and empathize with teams unfamiliar with the Foundation's ways.

HBO International Marketing

Game of Thrones Exhibition

Overview

PBJS (now Publicis Experiences) developed and produced the 2015 Game of Thrones Global Exhibition, taking an immersive, sharable experience across Europe on a six-city tour from London to Tel Aviv that engaged nearly 100,000 people.

My Role

Creative and design director of the exhibition space, including curation of all props and costumes. Co-creative director for all digital and social content. On the show's set in Belfast, I curated a collection of more than 200 props and costumes and placed each within the exhibition's content plan.

Key Learning

The exhibition embodied all that I love about experiential marketing because I got to do everything. Building an experience for hardcore fans set a high bar for subject matter expertise and authenticity, requiring painstaking attention to detail—every costume, every prop, every line had to be perfect.

Microsoft

Imagine Cup

Overview

Imagine Cup is a global competition that encourages computer science students to use their creativity, passion, and coding skills to create the next big breakthrough. Finalists from around the world travel to Seattle to compete in a two-day competition.

My Role

For three years, I served as lead writer and creative consultant, working with Microsoft's Developer & Platform Evangelism team to develop the approach to each year's event and write all stage and screen content. I evolved the event's format from a multi-hour stage production to a 30-minute event shot for online viewing.

Key Learning

Listening to execs, understanding their personalities and speech patterns, and writing for them in a way that helped them feel comfortable onstage. Plus, having empathy for international students competing in a high-pressure coding competition.
